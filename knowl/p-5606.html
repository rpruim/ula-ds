Notice that
\begin{equation*} \proj{\yvec}{\xvec} = \frac{\len{\yvec} \cos \theta}{\len{\xvec}} \xvec = \frac{\len{\xvec}\len{\yvec} \cos \theta}{\len{\xvec}^2} \xvec \text{.} \end{equation*}
This leads to our geometric definition of the dot product, which is similar to the peojection, but is (a) symmetric in \(\xvec\) and \(\yvec\text{,}\) (b) avoids the denominator, and (c) results in a scalar rather than a vector. The result will be easier to work with algebraically and also easier to compute.
