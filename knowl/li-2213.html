If \(A\) is a diagonal matrix, then \(\det(A)\) equals the product of the entries on the diagonal. A \(2 \by 2\) diagonal matrix represents a transformation that scales horizontally and vertically by the scaling factors on the diagonal. The area of the transformed unit square is the product of the diagonal entries. If exactly one of the two is negative, the orientation is reversed and the determinant will be negative.
