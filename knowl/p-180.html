The typical organization places observational units (usually referred to a as a case or a subject when the observational units are people) in rows and the variables recorded for each of them in columns. So if we have \(k\) numerical variables recorded for each of \(n\) observational units, we can store this information in an \(n \times k\) matrix. For categorical variables, we will need to convert the values into numbers.
