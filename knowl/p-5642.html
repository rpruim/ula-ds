By the definition of cosine, this means that
\begin{equation*} \frac{k \len{\xvec}}{\len{\yvec}} = \cos \theta \end{equation*}
where \(\len{\xvec}\) denotes the (Euclidean) length of the vector \(\xvec\) and \(\theta\) is the angle between the two vectors. Solving for \(k\text{,}\) we find that
\begin{equation*} k = \frac{\len{\yvec} \cos \theta}{\len{\xvec}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
So
\begin{equation*} \proj{\yvec}{\xvec} = \frac{\len{\yvec} \cos \theta}{\len{\xvec}} \xvec\text{.} \end{equation*}
