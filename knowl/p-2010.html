Any vector \(\xvec = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ \vdots \\ x_n\end{bmatrix}\) in \(\real^n\) can be written as
\begin{equation*} x_1 \evec_1 + x_2 \evec_2 + \cdots + x_n \evec_n\text{.} \end{equation*}
Since \(T\) is linear, we have
\begin{align*} T(\xvec) \amp = x_1 T(\evec_1) + x_2 T(\evec_2) + x_n T(\evec_n)\\ \amp = \begin{bmatrix} T(\evec_1) \amp T(\evec_2) \amp \cdots \amp T(\evec_n) \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ \amp \\ x_n \end{bmatrix} \text{.} \end{align*}
So \(T\) is a matrix transformation with matrix \(\begin{bmatrix} T(\evec_1) \amp T(\evec_2) \amp \cdots \amp T(\evec_n) \end{bmatrix} \text{.}\)
