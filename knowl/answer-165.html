Answer 5.1.4.9.1.

If \(A\) is not square, then \(A \xvec\) and \(\lambda \xvec\) have different dimensions, so they can’t be the same.
The big idea here is that by part a we can’t put \(\xvec\) on both sides of \(A \xvec = \lambda \uvec\text{.}\) But we could use different vectors on the left and right sides. This only become interesting if we add some constraints on the vectors, like requiring them to form bases of the row and column space of \(A\text{.}\) In fact, we can require even more: we can require that the bases be othogonal (each pair of vectors forms a right angle.). We will return to this idea in Chapter 7.
