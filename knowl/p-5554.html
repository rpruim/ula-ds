The Euclidean norm of an \(n\)-dimensional vector \(\xvec\) is
\begin{equation*} \len{\xvec} = \left(\sum_{i=1}^n (x_i^2\right)^{1/2} = \left(\sum_{i=1}^n |x_i^2|\right)^{1/2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
If we change the exponent from \(2\) to some other number \(p > 1\) we obtain an expression for the \(p\)-norm or \(L^p\) norm:
\begin{equation*} \len{\xvec}_p = \left(\sum_{i=1}^n |x_i^p|\right)^{1/p}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Notice that
\begin{equation*} \len{\xvec}_1 = \left(\sum_{i=1}^n |x_i|\right) \end{equation*}
is the sum of the absolute values of the components of \(\xvec\text{.}\)
