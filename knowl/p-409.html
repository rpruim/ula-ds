As we have seen, numpy treats vectors and matrices as 1-dimensional and 2-dimensional arrays (both created using np.array()). While mathematically 2-dimensional arrays (i.e, matrices) are the most important (and vectors can be treated like \(n \by 1\) matrices); computationally, there is no reason we need to stop at rows and columns. We could add additional dimensions. Higher dimensional arrays are often called tensors.
