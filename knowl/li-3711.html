Item e.
The following Python cell defines a command called inverse_power_eig that applies the power method to \(A^{-1}\text{.}\) That is, inverse_power_eig(A, x0, N) computes the vectors \(\xvec_k\text{,}\) where \(\xvec_{k+1} = A^{-1}\xvec_k\text{,}\) and multipliers \(\frac{1}{m_k}\text{,}\) which approximate the eigenvalue of \(A\text{.}\) Use it to find the eigenvalue of \(A=\left[\begin{array}{rr} -5.1 \amp 5.7 \\ -3.8 \amp 4.4 \\ \end{array}\right]\) having the smallest absolute value.
