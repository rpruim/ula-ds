Item 7.4.5.10.a.
True. Remembering that \(U\) is orthogonal, we have \(G = A^{\transpose}A=(U\Sigma V^{\transpose})^{\transpose}(U\Sigma V^{\transpose}) = V\Sigma^{\transpose} U^{\transpose} U \Sigma V^{\transpose} = V (\Sigma^{\transpose}\Sigma)V^{\transpose}\text{.}\) Since \(\Sigma^{\transpose}\Sigma\) is diagonal and \(V\) is orthogonal, this is an orthogonal diagonalization.
