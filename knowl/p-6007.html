In numpy, the transpose of a matrix A is given by np.transpose(A). Define the matrices
\begin{equation*} A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 0 \amp -3 \\ 2 \amp -2 \amp 1 \\ \end{bmatrix}, \hspace{6pt} B = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \amp -4 \amp 1 \\ 0 \amp 1 \amp 2 \\ \end{bmatrix}, \hspace{6pt} C= \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 0 \amp -3 \\ 2 \amp -2 \amp 1 \\ 3 \amp 2 \amp 0 \\ \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  1. Evaluate \((A+B)^{\transpose}\) and \(A^{\transpose}+B^{\transpose}\text{.}\) What do you notice about the relationship between these two matrices?
  2. What happens if you transpose a matrix twice; that is, what is \((A^{\transpose})^{\transpose}\text{?}\)
  3. Find \(\det(C)\) and \(\det(C^{\transpose})\text{.}\) What do you notice about the relationship between these determinants?
    1. Find the product \(AC\) and its transpose \((AC)^{\transpose}\text{.}\)
    2. Is it possible to compute the product \(A^{\transpose}C^{\transpose}\text{?}\) Explain why or why not.
    3. Find the product \(C^{\transpose}A^{\transpose}\) and compare it to \((AC)^{\transpose}\text{.}\) What do you notice about the relationship between these two matrices?
  5. What is the transpose of the identity matrix \(I\text{?}\)
  6. If a square matrix \(D\) is invertible, explain why you can guarantee that \(D^{\transpose}\) is invertible and why \((D^{\transpose})^{-1} = (D^{-1})^{\transpose}\text{.}\)
