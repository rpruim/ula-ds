We will motivate the defintion of the dot product by investigating the projection of a vector \(\yvec\) in the direction of a vector \(\xvec\text{,}\) which we will denote as \(\proj{\yvec}{\xvec}\text{.}\) The projection vector \(\proj{\yvec}{\xvec}\) is the scalar multiple \(k \xvec\) of \(\xvec\) that is as close as possible to \(\yvec\text{.}\) That means to find the projection vector, we must find the scalar \(k\) that makes the vector \(\yvec - k \xvec\) as short as possible.
