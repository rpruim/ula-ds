An \(n\by n\) matrix \(A\) is diagonalizable if and only if there is a basis of \(\real^n\) consisting of eigenvectors of \(A\text{.}\) In that case, we can find a diagonalization by letting the columns of \(P\) consist of eigenvectors of \(A\) and the diagonal entries of \(D\) be the associated eigenvalues.
