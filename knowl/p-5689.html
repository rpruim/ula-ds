We will motivate the defintion of the dot product by investigating the projection of a vector \(\bvec\) in the direction of a vector \(\vvec\text{,}\) which we will denote as \(\proj{\bvec}{\vvec}\text{.}\) The projection vector \(\proj{\bvec}{\vvec}\) is the scalar multiple \(k \vvec\) of \(\vvec\) that is as close as possible to \(\bvec\text{.}\) That means to find the projection vector, we must find the scalar \(k\) that makes the vector \(\bvec - k \vvec\) as short as possible.
