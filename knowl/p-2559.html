If \(A\) is an invertible matrix, then for any \(\bvec \in \real^n\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} A (A^{-1} \bvec) = (A A^{-1}) \bvec = \bvec\text{.} \end{equation*}
This implies that \(A \xvec = \bvec\) has a unique solution for every \(\bvec\text{,}\) namely \(\xvec = A^{-1} \bvec\text{.}\)
