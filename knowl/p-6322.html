This section introduced orthogonal sets and the projection formula that allows us to project vectors orthogonally onto a subspace.
  • Given an orthogonal set \(\wvec_1,\wvec_2,\ldots,\wvec_n\) that spans an \(n\)-dimensional subspace \(W\) of \(\real^m\text{,}\) the orthogonal projection of \(\bvec\) onto \(W\) is the vector in \(W\) closest to \(\bvec\) and may be written as
    \begin{align*} \bhat \amp = \proj{\bvec}{\wvec_1} + \proj{\bvec}{\wvec_2} + \cdots + \proj{\bvec}{\wvec_n} \\ \amp = \frac{\bvec\cdot\wvec_1}{\wvec_1\cdot\wvec_1}\wvec_1 + \frac{\bvec\cdot\wvec_2}{\wvec_2\cdot\wvec_2}\wvec_2 + \cdots + \frac{\bvec\cdot\wvec_n}{\wvec_n\cdot\wvec_n}\wvec_n\text{.} \end{align*}
  • If \(\uvec_1,\uvec_2,\ldots,\uvec_n\) is an orthonormal basis of \(W\) and \(Q\) is the matrix whose columns are \(\uvec_i\text{,}\) then the matrix \(P=QQ^{\transpose}\) projects vectors orthogonally onto \(W\text{.}\)
  • If the columns of \(Q\) form an orthonormal basis for an \(n\)-dimensional subspace of \(\real^m\text{,}\) then \(Q^{\transpose}Q=I_n\text{.}\)
  • An orthogonal matrix \(Q\) is a square matrix whose columns form an orthonormal basis. In this case, \(QQ^{\transpose}=Q^{\transpose}Q = I\) so that \(Q^{-1} = Q^{\transpose}\text{.}\)
