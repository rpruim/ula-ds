Exercise 1.

  1. Show that \(\xtilde \cdot \ytilde = \xvec \cdot \ytilde = \xtilde \cdot \yvec\text{.}\) So to form the dot product of two demeaned vectors, it suffices to demean one of them and not the other.
  2. Let \(\xvec = \fourvec1326\) and let \(\yvec = \fourvec524{-3}\text{.}\) Compute \(\xtilde \cdot \ytilde\text{,}\) \(\xvec \cdot \ytilde\text{,}\) and \(\xtilde \cdot \yvec\) and show that you get the same result each time.
  3. Show that \(\xvec \cdot \yvec\) gives a different result. (So it is important to demean at least one of the vectors.)
